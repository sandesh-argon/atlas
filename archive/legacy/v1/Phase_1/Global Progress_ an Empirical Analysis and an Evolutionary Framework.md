Global Progress: an Empirical Analysis and an Evolutionary Framework

**By Francis Heylighen & Jay Bernheim** 

Introduction

*  Progress is defined as increase in global quality of life (QOL)  
  * Measured through life satisfaction questionnaires   
  * Social, economic, and psychological factors that affect QOL including wealth, health, security, knowledge, freedom and equality.  
  * Progress Objectively occurs

* Modernity \-\> belief in rationality, progress, and science  
  * Modernity helps us discover truth about the world and ourselves which fuels technological progress and releases us from the shackles of ignorance.  
  * The ongoing accumulation of scientific knowledge directly leads to economic and social progress.  
  * The more we push the bounds of science the better the world becomes

* Post-Modern \-\> Knowledge as a set of perspectives   
  * They found that if the world is only one way as per science it means others who believe differently are wrong, which leads to intolerance and violence  
  * They believe in the relativity of good and evil   
  * The modern Western way of life is not objectively superior to the way of life of more “primitive cultures”, either pre industrialization or in current third world countries.

* Publicity of negative events causes people to exaggerate the negative state of the world causing people to believe development simply leads to a more difficult life. Envying the people of the pre-industrial era.

* This paper argues that both the **relativistic and pessimistic views are inadequate and that currently we objectively have a better life than ever before**, and this progress will continue. This will be proven using statistics and scientific data with a theoretical framework.

* However the paper departs from **Newtonian WorldView** in 3 Ways  
  * Holism or emergentism \- **the whole is more than the sum of its parts**, they may interact with each other **synergistically or antagonistically**.  
  * **Includes non material mental factors** such as intelligence, knowledge, goal directedness and feeling.  
  * **Evolution \- systems are not static entities**, rather a survival of a fittest for different factors

Global Quality of Life

* Newtonian world view reduces progress to material growth, accumulation of knowledge or a linear combination of such factors.

* What is considered good and evil is intrinsically subjective. Is material growth growth if it comes at the cost of resource depletion and pollution? Because it cannot be derived from objective facts \- it is better to define progress as the **increase in the subjectivity experienced quality of life for the world population.**  
  * QOL can be defined as overall happiness or satisfaction with one’s life. A property that is more than the sum of its components, while someone may not be able to decisively tell the organ, muscle, or nerve that is causing them pain. They are able to accurately assess the pain as a whole. It’s the same when asked to judge their own QOL.

Measuring QOL

* There exists a number of standardized item-based questionnaires for evaluating QOL. The SF-36 test breaks the problem into different components such as bodily pain, social functioning, and mental health. However the weights of each of these categories on overall QOL is largely individual, and these factors do not correspond with each other linearly; as when one factor is missing it tends to be the most important.

* The **global QOL questionnaire known as ACSA** (Anamnestic Comparative Self Assessment) fixes this. By having people evaluate their overall feeling by comparing their present situation with the best and worst situation they have ever felt in their life.   
  * The main advantage is that it eliminates the social desirability bias, where participants are pressured into saying what’s expected of them from their culture. It also avoids peer relativity, so they don’t compare with peers in their group \- just themselves.  
  * The downside is that it’s still based on the relativity of the participants' best/worst experiences. If there is a way to level emotional saturation, a point where a person cannot feel more happy or sad in a particular moment then this test would be comparable, if not another test must complement this one.

Is Happiness Relative or Absolute?

* Level of Adaptation  Theory  
  * People always aspire to improve what they have. They choose their level of aspiration to be higher than what they have now, and it continually increases with forward progress.   
  * Series of small improvements is likely to be more satisfying than one big jump, followed by a level of deterioration.  
  * This measurement would tell us very little about people’s overall situation and make it difficult to compare, because it is purely relative to person   
  * However, all existing measures of QOL have strong correlations to objective factors such as wealth, health, and success/failure.

* The combination of the relative theory (one above) and an objective theory is most likely to be correct. **“Thus the objective criteria would determine an invariant base level of QOL, which is modulated by a changing subjunctive appreciation.”**  
  * This model follows **Maslow’s Theory of basic needs.** Where deficiency needs (such as food, water and shelter) and growth needs (learning and self-actualization) must be met to some degree in order to achieve higher levels of satisfaction.  
  * The satisfaction of deficiency needs stops at a given saturation level, while the satisfaction of growth needs imply a continuous increase in aspiration levels.  
  * **Overall satisfaction can then be reliably measured**, as we can **measure satisfaction of deficiency by what degree it has been eliminated;** **and then the satisfaction of growth can be attained by psychological tests** that measure the degree to which people feel capable to reach their aspirations (regardless of what they are).  
  * Self-actualization can be reinterpreted as the “perceived competence to satisfy basic needs” \- a feeling of control over one’s own situation. The subject’s perceived capacity for maintaining or improving that situation.   
  * **Happiness is the sense of a preponderance of positive feelings is then merely biological signal that everything is OK**

* Should QOL scores be computed for the individual or a society as a whole?  
  * QOL for a society does not seem to merely be the sum average of QOL scores for all its members. A society might achieve a high QOL for the average citizen with a suffering minority of poor people.   
  * **The utilitarian ideal of “the greatest happiness to the greatest number” remains ambiguous if, and must be further clarified if we wish to define progress for the world as a whole.** 

Correlates of QOL

* Factors of QOL can be found via statistical analysis of different variables which are correlated to QOL. Correlation does not imply causation, however the correlated factor can be seen as an indicator for QOL. That means that if we see that factor go up we can expect, in general, for QOL to go up.

* Ruut Veenhoven and his associates at University of Rotterdam have compiled an extensive World Database of Happiness. Which contain hundreds of polls akin to Global QOL. While worded differently they have been standardized so they may be compared, and all ask the similar question “How satisfied are you with your current situation”.  
  * However these questionnaires have similar problems to the ACSA with cultural biases \- further study is needed.   
  * Life satisfaction turns out to have strong positive correlations to factors that we intuitively consider as good

**Physical Variables**

* Most obvious factor correlated with QOL is **physical well-being or health**. Indicated by measures of life expectancy and child mortality. Which is a sum predictor of health as adequate nutrition and quality and quantity of health care directly correlate to it.   
* **Average caloric intake is correlated with life satisfaction**, **up to a point** where the subject is expected to be satiated. This is expected of a deficiency need.   
* Countries where nutrition is good experience a growth spurt in its newest generation. Tallness is one less obvious factor in this regard. 

**Economic Variables**

* Countries’ wealth is usually measured by GNP (Gross national product) per inhabitant. However, this is a bad measure in terms of QOL as it is not really a measure of wealth, but of the money being transacted. Someone could have a ton of money, buy nothing, and then not contribute to GDP. **Purchasing power, while more difficult to measure, is a much better measurement for QOL than GDP.**  
* Correlation of wealth to QOL diminishes once a certain wealth level is reached (about the level of Mexico), meaning wealth is a deficiency need. **Once people have sufficient money, further earnings contribute little to QOL**. Within rich Western countries there is practically no difference in QOL between rich and (relatively) poor people due to wealth.

 **Social Variables**

* People’s social position and the organization of the society in which they live play an important role in their QOL. Veenhoven notes a **clear correlation between average happiness of a country and social equality** (measured by equality between the sexes and equality in incomes \- distribution of wealth).  
* There is also a correlation with personal and economic freedom. Where freedom does not reach a saturation level, implying it is a growth need. Another set of factors helping QOL is the general infrastructure of the society. Fair and easily enforceable laws, and the development of paths for the distribution of goods and services (road, railways, and airports).  
* **Peacefulness, security and political stability** are important requirements for a high QOL.

**Cognitive Variables**

* Veenhoven found that **education (measured by literacy and school enrollment) has the largest positive correlation to QOL**. As did media attendance.  
* The collective intelligence or social capital of a society \- its capacity for efficiently distributing and co-ordinated effort \- is a more subtle correlate for QOL.  The unwritten rules that individuals follow with their interactions with others is an example of social cognition. A country in which there is little trust and high corruption will have a low collective intelligence.

**Personality Variables**

* An individual’s general character and outlook on life correlates strongly with happiness. **Life-satisfaction tends to be higher among people with good mental health, social assertiveness, and empathy**. This goes along with Maslow’s description of the highest level, self-actualization, **openness to experience, psychological resilience, and internal control beliefs**.   
* It boils down to the principle that happy people are those most able to control their environment. Confirming the evolutionary interpretation of QOL.

**Unrelated Variables**

* Suicide rate, distrust in institutions, trust in family members, population density, population growth, and religiosity have zero effect on QOL.   
* The importance of industry typically increases when a country develops mostly out of the agricultural stage, and then decreases again when it turns into a service-based economy. 

 **Conclusion**

* All factors mentioned before (wealth, safety, health, education, freedom, equality, etc.) are **mutually correlated**. They all tend to go up or down together.  
* This presence of correlation gives us objective criterion for deciding which particular variables are good indicators of progress. And **by selecting only the correlated variables we can make an “index of progress”.** Which would be safe from being either arbitrarily or ideologically biased as it’s so empirically supported.   
* HDI questionnaire is at present the most reliable indicator of progress.

Progress in QOL

* Very few longitudinal studies in which changes in QOL have been measured over prolonged intervals  
* These studies are prone to distortion as people tend to increase their aspiration levels as their situation gets better.   
* The **lack of direct observation of QOL evolution can be made up for by the massive amount of data in the correlates**  \- from life expectancy, wealth, level of education, etc.

**Physical Progress**

* Since 1950 there has been a **consistent increase in life-expectancy** for both poor and developed countries, with the gap between them continually closing.   
* Life expectancy is the most reliable measure of the physical component of QOL, and the one most closely related to the biological concept of fitness.   
* Other variables that indicate progress are **calories available per head of the population, child mortality rate, and tallness**.

**Economic Progress**

* Average wealth increase in almost all countries is well documented. **Poverty on world-level has decreased from 70% to 30% in 2000\.**  
* **Productivity tends to be a constant linear line of 2-3% a year and is unphased by the boom-bust cycles of the economy.** Increased productivity means it takes less resources and labor to produce the same amount of goods.   
* The **richer people become the less they need to spend on basic resources such as food, energy and materials.** Contrary to the limited resource ideology, all 5 natural resources expected to be close to exhaustion had effectively become cheaper from 1980-1990.  
* While some resources are undeniably finite it **does not mean that we will ever suffer from their depletion.** Resources are continually used **more efficiently** so that even the massive reserves we have are becoming used less and less. Even if we get to the point of depletion, resources are already being **recycled and more prominently substituted** with other inexhaustible resources (nuclear, solar, wind, etc.).

**Social Progress**

* While there are less clear statistics on social progress, it does seem that **over the longer term there is an increasing equality between sexes world-wide and income** (although the inverse for the latter is true among developing countries).  
* There has been a spectacular increase in democracy and general freedom of expression world-wide.

**Security Progress**

* Number of people killed though accidents and homicide have decreased steadily. Even with automobile accidents taking precedent for the biggest killer, deaths per mile driven is decreasing, and even the absolute deaths have begun to decrease.   
* **Peeters (1979) argues economic development first increases the risks of large scale war,as more money and more lethal technologies become available for building up military power, but then decreases as a higher level of social and cognitive development makes the public understand how much there is to be lost and how little to be gained by waging war.**   
* Risk of war would become quite small once a country has reached a development level comparable to mid-century Europe.   
* There is a tendency towards decrease over the last few decades. **The end of the cold war has made it very unlikely we will witness anything like a 3rd World War**. 

**Cognitive Progress**

* There is plenty of data that show a strong and consistent improvement. Illiteracy has virtually disappeared in the developed world and is quickly receding in the developing countries. Education is continually becoming more important to people as they are spending more time in school.  
* This along with the explosion of communication media has made accessing information much easier.  
* **Intelligence, measured by IQ, has been undergoing a constant increase of 3 points per decade for the 20 or some countries for which data is available. This is known as the Flynn effect.**   
* Kohlberg’s levels of moral development \- which determine to what degree people are capable of autonomously making decisions which are for the good of society without relying purely on the fear of punishment or adherence to a rigid set of moral or religious commands.

**Psychological Progress**

* According to Masolw’s theory of self-actualization, **increased satisfaction of basic needs should lead to an improvement of mental health** (decrease in neuroticism, more openness to experience, self-confidence, and the belief in one’s capability to change things for the better).  
* As there is no data to test this conjecture we can **intuitively understand** that people are more open-minded, self-confident, and autonomous than a century ago. The general attitude towards life seems more relaxed and spontaneous.   
* **Rigid control of behavior and emotions has seen less of a need as living conditions are much easier, and has created room for acceptance and expression of different feelings, emotions and desires.**   
* This has reduced psycho pathogenic repressions and increased the capacity to feel pleasant feelings. Some mental disorders such as hysteria have seemed to disappear.   
* However, **other disorders like anxiety and depression have become more common.** 

**Conclusion**

* Practically every factor correlated to QOL shows a consistent, ongoing improvement over the last century.   
* The general development can also be exemplified by the probability of accidental death. This factor is both strongly correlated with QOL and consistently decreasing with no one single obvious cause for the decline.  
* Over time these increases in QOL have decreased the probability of serious misfortune, and thereby improve the control people have over their fate.

Negative Trends

* We are definitely not living in the best of possible worlds. Many things are still much worse than we would like them to be, and some things are effectively deteriorating.  
* As progress has raised our standards and expectations, we now pay attention to problems that used to be ignored or considered normal in earlier periods \- such as poverty, pollution or child abuse \- which is a healthy attitude.  
* However, there exists an inherent bias that exaggerates all negative developments and diminishes the positive ones. 

**Good News is No News**

* Failure and catastrophes simply receive much more attention. Most of the time, things go the way they are supposed to go, because that is what expectations are based on.  
* The normal expectation is that things either remain the same or slowly improve. A situation only gains attention if it deviates from this path \- either deteriorating or improving spectacularly.  
* Positive developments are the accumulated effect of the sustained efforts of many people \- they merely require the continuation of activities.  
* From the point of view of survival, not noticing a positive trend carries little risk. Not paying attention to an imminent danger, on the other hand, can be fatal.   
* Bad news is acute and good news is chronic. This effect is strongly amplified by the media.  
* The larger the deviation from the normal situation, the more newsworthy it is.  
* The pessimistic mood and reach of the media have increased together because of this  
* The occurrence of killings, crime and war in movies, novels and TV series is immensely greater than its actual occurrence in the outside world. This leaves the public with a strongly distorted view of the true situation.

**The Motivation Aspect of Bad News**

* The attention of the public is always drawn towards shortcomings  
* This is something to be applauded. But because of the negative atmosphere people often react with fatalism \- which is the opposite of the desired effect.  
* When the environment seems so unsavable, people tend to feel that they could not do anything to help it. So they stop doing their part (even though it’s small, collectively will turn an environment around) and further push the spiral of deterioration.  
* Instead it was found that instead of tackling the really harsh problems, the most successful attempts to reverse this downward spiral have been focused on combating more conspicuous signs of deterioration, such as littering, misdemeanors and petty crime.  
* This “zero tolerance” policy where the superficial problems are solved first caused more serious crimes to deteriorate as well because the inhabitants regained a feeling of control over their neighborhood, and thereby the motivation to work for the further improvement of their situation.  
* By fixing the atmosphere for inhabitants (the litter, aura, and attitudes of those around them) they report a better sense of safety even though that hasn’t actually changed. It’s how people perceive their world that matters \- not necessarily how it actually is in order to motivate them.  
* In the same way the media over reporting of bad news has made people feel hopeless of making an impact. A more realistic (which is more positive) portrayal of the situation will garner enthusiasm for further improvement.   
* **The best combination to motivate people is still a combination of the carrot and the stick: on the one hand you must show them the dire consequences if they do not act, on the other hand you must show them how large the rewards will be if they do act. This latter, positive, approach seems to be neglected, contributing to an overall atmosphere of gloom and doom. And will motivate them to act instead of staying stagnant and hopeless.**  As long as they do not contribute to the negative they are doing good, instead this will help them realize that they can contribute to the positive. 

**Environmental Conditions**

* Fuelling most of the prophecies of doom, is the perceived deterioration of the environment. Because ecological bullets are side effects of economic growth, many people think that material progress necessarily goes together with environmental deterioration.   
* Although industrial pollution negatively affects our health, these are much smaller than the positive effects **brought about by medical advances and a higher standard of living. The life expectancy statistic is a bullet proof reasoning.**  
* It has been estimated that the elimination of all cancer deaths due to pollutants, additives and nuclear materials would increase life expectancy by a mere 90 days.   
* People tend to overestimate the dangers of artificial poisons, when in reality their danger is simply a by-product of their intended use (to make people healthier). While natural toxins in plants have been selectively bred by natural selection to be as harmful as possible.  
* **Although pollution on the world scale is still increasing, there is a clear trend towards decrease in the developed countries.**   
* As economy and technology advance, more money and better techniques become available for minimizing pollution. As the general quality of life increases, people are less willing to undergo the effects of pollution and more motivated to enjoy a pleasant, natural environment.   
* Discovering the ozone hole has actually led to a success story as it caused an international treaty for the phasing out of CFCs (what causes ozone depletion). The rate of this ozone depletion has severely dropped since then and is supposed to stop completely in the next decade \- predicted to reach a max size a few years from now and then diminish thereafter. Its effect on health in the form of increased frequency of skin cancer, is likely to be much smaller than the effect of increased sunbathing or use of UV tanning lamps.   
* Global warming and the effects of carbon dioxide production have yet to be slowed at all as it’s increasing so rapidly. **However, not only has the early gone through hotter and colder phases than this naturally, but the warming of the earth can have an overall positive effect as humanity has seen to have done better with warmer weather (increased rain leading to increased crop yield).** Because of these uncertainties concerning its intensity, effects, or possible ways to avoid it, that concern and further research are in order, but pessimism seems inappropriate.  
* The fast reduction of tropical forests, rain forests and the concurring loss of biodiversity is concerning. However, the earth has also seen much worse fluctuation with an extinction of 96% of life and still managed to bounce back. Not only that extreme case, but in the countries where forests are being decimated, when they reach a higher economic level they will begin having forest management for their sake and for the biodiversity. **With the growing number of natural reserves and increased protection of wildlife it is unlikely that we will ever come near to such drastic levels of extinction.**   
* Finally the population explosion problem has lost much of its urgency. As population growth in developed countries is essentially zero and in developing countries rapidly decreasing. **It’s expected to stabilize at about 10.3 Billion in 2100\.** In only agriculturally centered countries that are densely populated will this be a serious problem, while in almost all other cases an increase in population, in the long run, leads to more growth \- while temporarily resources run low, they cause an increased demand that promotes innovation. **Both Japan and the Netherlands have shown how an increasing population can lead to increased productivity.**

**The Pace of Life**

* There is an ever accelerating speed of change in our present society. **Because innovation is taking pace at such a breath-taking pace, no one can really keep up with it.** Even researchers and scientists are overwhelmed by the changes in their domain.  
* People need to constantly revise their skills in order to adapt to the changing circumstances, but not everybody can cope as well with this need for constant re-education causing the problems of unemployment and increasing wealth gap in Western countries.  
* As traditional jobs are disappearing like industrial and agricultural employees need to adapt to a much more information driven society that is much more intellectually demanding.   
* Too much change will put a strain on people and organizations leading to the **“future shock” syndrome \- where the rapid changes of modern life may develop a state of helplessness and inadequacy,** where they simply cannot cope with new developments anymore.   
* They have found a positive correlation between change and physical illness. Change being both positive like marriage or child birth and negative like divorce or job loss.   
* Change affects not only our physical state but our mental state. The first reaction to something new can be positive when novelty elects curiosity and wonder, or negative where lack of understanding triggers confusion or fear. If a person does not find an adequate response to the novel stimuli, he or she will experience loss of control and distress.   
* **There are three main categories for animals when it comes to stressful situations. Fight, flight, or fright.** In people the responses seem to indicate that they are rooted from a long process of failed attempts to control stressful situations.   
  * Fight \- corresponds to anger and aggression. In people it seems to be apathy, which cannot be sustained for very long.  
  * Flight \- corresponds to fear and anxiety. In people that seems to be depression. Fear and anxiety can be constantly present. Fear being directed at a frightened target, while anxiety is generalized as the feeling that something bad will happen.  
  * Fright \- where the animal freezes in the face of danger. In people this seems to be despair.   
* Not only individuals but a society as a whole can undergo these negative effects of too rapid change. **Aggression can be seen as vandalism, hooliganism and mass shootings (in a more extreme case). While depression and despair can be seen as the “burn-out” syndrome or drug addiction to get away from the situation as a whole.**  
* The most common neurosis present in society is anxiety. With the record use of anxiolytic drugs and the disproportionate reactions to far-away threats people are more anxious than ever before. Which is often brought out and magnified by the media’s bad news bias.  
* Anxiety is apparent in the growing feeling of insecurity. Fear of aggression tends to increase more quickly than actual crime rates. The turmoil in the labor market makes future earnings very unpredictable.   
* The acceleration of change is accompanied by an increase in the information needed to keep up with all these developments. Resulting in **“Information Fatigue Syndrome”** adding to the stress caused by the need to constantly adapt to a changing situation.  
* **The biggest problem facing present-day society is not that there is too little progress, but rather too much of it. Our mind and our physiology is not fit to cope with such a great amount of change.**   
* Because this information overload is hard to define, people tend to use easily recognizable causes such as unemployment, pollution, crime, etc. as scapegoats for the real feeling of their dissatisfaction \- **there is simply too much progress/change causing the anxiety they feel.**  
* We can only expect for progress to **further accelerate which will only be limited by our capacities to cope.** It is obvious that this evolution **threatens to seriously reduce our QOL.**  
* We can predict that a new kind of equilibrium can be reached, characterized by the “information society” or the “global brain”. In the meantime we can only try to better manage change. One obvious counteraction could consist in consciously **postponing innovations until their usefulness has been fully proven and their implementation stabilized.**   
* We must also implement **“permanent education”** such that there does not become an information gap. A third one is to investigate and develop **technologies to more intelligently process and filter the available information.**

Mechanisms of Progress

**Natural Selection**

* Why is there progress rather than regress? The process of evolution is inherently progressive, whether it be the organisms themselves, the physical systems, technological inventions, social organizations or simply ideas. **Evolution proceeds by the variation of a given design and the selection of the most fit variants**.   
* Fitness refers to the **probability that a given design will survive and be reproduced**. Fit variants by definition will become more numerous while unfit ones disappear. Therefore, the **average fitness of a population can only increase or remain the same, but never decrease**. The only matter of debate is whether fitter means better \- and as long as death or extinction is inherently bad and survival or growth are inherently good then fit must mean good.   
* The quality of life can be seen as an extension of the original biological concept of fitness. Thus, the continuing increase of fitness must incite progress.  
* There are ways in which fitness increases in an absolute sense, while environments due vary, fitness can then be a measure of how well an organism can handle a changing environment. The larger the range, the fitter the organism. This type of fitness promotes generalism rather than specialism.

**The Growth of Knowledge**

* Progress through natural selection tends to be **very slow and inefficient**. Although it guarantees that negative developments won’t last it is usually accompanied by great suffering and a loss of resources.  
* **Cultural evolution has developed a number of shortcuts** for the tortuous process of blind variation and natural selection. Knowledge selects the most fruitful actions from potential actions without actually destroying systems or organisms in its process. Because of knowledge we can substitute blind variation with some level of foresight.  
* **Knowledge in principle is always incomplete**, no theory can make perfectly accurate predictions in all domains. That is why sometimes decisions made with the best intent and “knowledge” turn out disastrous.   
* **Our difficulty to anticipate effects (feedforward) is to some degree compensated by our capability to correct mistakes after the fact (feedback).** When you observe that things are going the opposite direction you can take counteraction to correct for the mistake. Many times the origin of the problem is of little consequence, as you can act based on the outputs to correct the issue.  
* The feedforward and feedback process is the classic cybernetic paradigm of control. **Even with deficient knowledge, progress can be made towards the chosen goals**, and disturbances can be compensated, whatever their origin.   
* Feedback does require at least an awareness of the problem. Where the current situation deviates from the ideal.   
* **A system that is incapable to solve the problems that threaten its survival will fall to the laws of natural selection and eventually disintegrate and be replaced by a system that can cope better.**

**Virtuous Cycles**

* The quick expansion of knowledge to some degree explains why progress is more visible now. But the explosion of social and economic development requires more explanation.   
* The **correlates that contribute to an individual's QOL also contribute to global QOL or the fitness of humanity.**   
* **Progress in one domain will make society more competent to push for progress in another domain creating a virtuous cycle of progress producing more progress.**  
* The growth of knowledge affects all other domains as a catalyst for productivity, same goes for wealth, health, and security. **All are linked together so the improvement of one will almost always lead to the improvement of the others.**   
* This idea can be broken down to the domains themselves, when one part of the domain does better as does the other. **Progress feeds on progress, thus continuously promoting its own development.** When one person, country, or economy improves it helps the rest of the world. 

**Summary and Conclusion**

* This paper has **defined progress** in a universally acceptable, culture-independent manner and has shown that **progress defined this way effectively occurs**.  
* Using extensive data from the “World Database of Happiness”, it allowed them to find what variables are actually linked to QOL. **The results confirm the values that most people intuitively hold: health, wealth, security, knowledge, freedom and equality all seem to contribute to our feeling of well-being.**  
* Pollution, global change, population growth, speed of innovation, and information overload has all been touched on in this paper. **Concluding that while all of these problems are very real \- their effect on QOL is much less than what is usually portrayed and that there improvement is unmistakably there.**   
* While the problems of too rapid change and information fatigue are more subtle and hardly researched, **they are the biggest threat to global QOL and are likely the root of our present society’s wide-spread anxiety.**  
* Unfortunately the t**endency to worry is amplified by a needless media bias towards bad news**. This is where much of the resulting pessimism and despair could be **avoided by a more realistic \- and therefore more positive portrayal of the global situation.**  
* Such a progressive worldview would replace the mechanistic picture, in which we are separate, atomized individuals governed by deterministic laws, with the notion that **we are active and creative participants in a global evolution towards the greater good for the greater number.**   
* **Natural selection on its own is already sufficient to explain why positive developments survive and develop while negative ones disappear**. However, the explosion of progress is not purely due to natural selection. **The growth of knowledge allows us to more efficiently anticipate and control the results of our actions, The resulting acceleration in development is further boosted by the virtuous cycles, where progress in one domain facilitates progress in other domains.**   
* The best guarantee for continuing success is an open mind and pragmatic attitude, unrestricted by dogmas or prejudices.
